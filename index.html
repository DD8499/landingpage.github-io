In local language "Chorwad" means Unpredictable Sand. This Beach is considered to be dangerous because sand on the Beach and inside sea suddenly sinks and there have been several accidents here. That could be one of the reason that the Holiday Camp has been shut down. You can also see here the Chorwad Palace which belonged to Nawab of Junagadh.

Chorwad, 20 kilometers from Veraval and 70 kilometers from Junagarh, can be reached by air (Keshod, the nearest airport, is only 35 kilometers away), by road and by rail (the nearest railway station is at Veraval)

Chorwad is one of the popular beaches on the Gujarat border. Once the royal summer retreat of the pleasure-loving Nawabs of Junagarh, the imposing Chorwad Palace and the lovely expanse of beach before it combine to make Chorwad unique. Today, Chorwad lays claim to being India's only beach palace resort-the piece de resistance of Gujarat's beaches.
Chorvad has been since the earliest days famous for its betel gardens, and the flavour of Chorvad betel is supposed to be very superior, and it is largely exported not only inland but also by sea.
